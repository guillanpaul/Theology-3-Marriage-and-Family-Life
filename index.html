A Question to Sit With

If you were told that you only had 3 months to live, would you live any differently? What would you do with the remaining days of your life?

Steve Jobs: "If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?"

Steve Jobs is an example of a person who found his calling and mission in life so that he could die happy doing what he loved. Reflecting on death, rather than being morbid, can clarify what truly matters and help save — or reorient — a life.

The Power of Purpose

What makes one's life rich in poverty and another unfulfilling in abundance? The answer may be found in the seriousness of purpose. In fact, our survival, both individually and collectively, depends on it.

Changing Concepts of Calling

Calling is defined as fulfilling a specific life role for the greater good — a call one is chosen to answer. It also extends beyond religious or career contexts: for example, the calling to parenthood and child-rearing.

Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Motivation

Intrinsic Motivation

Refers to the pursuit of some activity for its own sake. Intrinsically motivated activities are rewarding and satisfying in their own right.

Extrinsic Motivation

Refers to the pursuit of some activity for some external reward — such as approval/praise, monetary incentives, and recognition or honor. Extrinsically motivated activities are a means to an end.

Sources of Intrinsic Motivation

  • Love someone unconditionally
  • Do something you love
  • Learn something new
  • Do something kind
  • Pursue an ideal beyond self-interest
Duncker's Candle Problem: Fix a lit candle on a wall so that the candle wax won't drip onto the table below, using only a candle, a book of matches, and a box of thumbtacks. (Solution: empty the box of thumbtacks, tack the box to the wall as a holder, and set the candle inside it — a classic illustration of how thinking beyond an object's obvious function unlocks intrinsically motivated, creative problem-solving.)
Approach vs. Avoidance Motivation

Approach Motivation

Primarily motivated to achieve some positive goals and outcomes.

Avoidance Motivation

Primarily motivated to avoid some negative outcomes, such as failure and stress.
Going through life with an avoidance orientation tends to create more stress and anxiety and less life satisfaction. These two types of life orientations tend to result in different kinds of life goals.
Meaning vs. Happiness Motivation

Meaning Orientation

More likely linked to intrinsic motivation and approach motivation.

Happiness Orientation

More likely linked to extrinsic motivation.

Vocation — A Call to Holiness
What is your life calling?
▼
What is Vocation?

The word vocation comes from the Latin vocare — meaning "to call."

Definition 1

A summons or strong inclination to a particular state or course of action — especially a divine call to the religious life.

Definition 2

An entry into the priesthood or a religious order.

Definition 3

The work in which a person is employed: occupation.

Definition 4

The persons engaged in a particular occupation. — Merriam-Webster
Vocation is not a profession or career where you think "What do I want to be?" — it is a calling of "What does God want me to be?"
What are the Different Vocations?
1

Single Life / Single Blessedness

People who are committed to living the single life. They love God and serve others — not committed to one person. They can love everyone just as Jesus did.

Gifts & Talents: ability to serve others, equal love for all, love of God.

2

Ordained Priesthood

Jesus supports his followers personally through ordained priesthood. The priestly vocation is a call to serve and a call to celibate love. Roles: Popes, Bishops, Deacons, Priests.

Gifts & Talents: ability to serve others' needs, commit to celibacy, guide and help others, become closer to God.

3

Religious Life

Following Jesus and the Gospel. Requires making the three evangelical vows: Poverty, Chastity, Obedience.

Gifts & Talents: dedication to God, prayer, holiness, living life the way Jesus did. Roles: Religious brothers/sisters, deacon, bishop, priest.

4

Married Life

Marriage is a vocation as you must commit and devote yourself to the married relationship. Jesus restores God's original plan and purpose for marriage.

God's two purposes: the sexual expression of creativity, and the procreation of children. Marriage is a sacrament that guides and strengthens couples.

Gifts & Talents: Loyalty, Trust, Honesty.

How do we discern our Vocation?

Discernment through Prayer — a process of prayer that helps us determine what God intends for us. It calls us to look at many sides of decisions, considering all different alternatives, and involves praying over a decision and asking for guidance from the Holy Spirit.

Steps of Discernment

  • Step 1: Find a quiet place to pray. Relax, find a comfortable position, close your eyes, and become aware of God's presence.
  • Step 2: Select a special word that makes you think of God and recite it to keep distractions away.
  • Step 3: Thank the Lord, tell Jesus of your love for Him, ask the Holy Spirit to remain with you, then slowly recite an Our Father.
Other sources for prayer: (1) Sacred Scripture, (2) Liturgy of the Church, (3) Practice of Virtues. Prayer is a dialogue with God — the most important part is listening to what God has to say.

Scriptural Calls

  • John 1:35–50 — Call of the First Disciples
  • Mark 10:17–22 — Call of the Rich Young Man
  • Matthew 10:1–40 — Call of the Twelve

Catholic Sexual Ethics
Foundations of Human Sexuality · Chastity · Offenses
▼
What are the Foundations of Human Sexuality?

1. Created Male & Female in God's Image

God is love, and He created humanity for love and communion. Being male or female is part of our God-given identity. Sexuality is integral to the human person and relates to our capacity to love, form relationships, and procreate.

2. Equal Dignity & Complementarity

Man and woman are equal in dignity but different in their roles and gifts. Their differences are complementary, meant for mutual support and the flourishing of family life.
What is Chastity?

Chastity is the integration of sexuality into the whole person — body and soul. It ensures inner unity and harmony in expressing love. (CCC 2337)

Self-Mastery

Discipline over passions, choosing what is good.

Virtue of Temperance

Chastity moderates desire through reason.

A Lifelong Effort

Requires daily commitment and prayer.

Form of Love

Chastity leads to genuine friendship and self-giving love.
The chaste person is not governed by his or her emotions. Rather, they understand the meaning of sexual feelings and appropriate behaviors. Chaste self-possession brings true freedom and peace. (CCC 2339 — "apprenticeship in self-mastery")
What are the Forms of Chastity by State of Life?

Married Couples

Live conjugal chastity.

Single People

Practice continence.

Consecrated Celibates

Offer their sexuality to God.

Engaged Couples

Live chastely as a preparation for marriage.
What are the Offenses Against Chastity? (6th & 9th Commandments)
1

Lust

A disordered or inordinate desire for or enjoyment of sexual pleasure.

2

Masturbation

Use of sexuality in a self-centered way, apart from its purpose in love and life.

3

Fornication

Sexual relations outside marriage.

4

Pornography

Consists in removing real or simulated sexual acts from the intimacy of the partners in order to display them deliberately to third parties.

5

Prostitution

Reducing the person to an instrument of sexual pleasure.

6

Rape

The forcible violation of the sexual intimacy of another person.

7

Homosexual Acts

Not ordered toward life or complementarity; considered intrinsically disordered. However, persons with same-sex attraction must be treated with respect, compassion, and are called to live chastely.

The Sixth Commandment isn't just about adultery — it upholds the holiness of sexuality. Sexuality is a gift meant to be expressed in love, within the bond of marriage. Chastity protects the dignity of the human person and leads to authentic love and communion.
What are the Purposes of Sexuality in Marriage?

1. Good of the Spouses

Unity, fidelity, joy — sexuality reflects total self-giving, open to life and love.

2. Procreation

Openness to life. The marital act is noble, honorable, a sign of joy, unity, and mutual enrichment — a participation in God's creative love.
These two purposes cannot be separated without harming marriage. The marital act must be both unitive and procreative by its very nature.

Human Sexuality
Fully Man, Fully Woman · Unitive & Procreative · Chastity
▼
What is Human Sexuality?

"Sexuality is a fundamental component of human personality, one of its modes of being, of manifestation, of communicating with others, of feeling, of expressing and of living human love."

— Truth and Meaning of Human Sexuality

Human sexuality should be founded upon a sound Christian anthropology, which describes the human person as:

A being at once corporeal and spiritual (CCC 362) Made in the image of God (CCC 355) With complementarity and equality of the sexes (CCC 355/9)
Man is a sexual being. To be human is to be sexual in one's very being. Our sexuality affects many, if not all, of our day-to-day actions. The gift of sexuality comes from God — who is both relational and social in and of Himself. The love of the Trinity is so strong that it has to create. Thus, love and sex should always be connected.
What are the Two Dimensions of Human Sexuality?

The Church teaches that human sexual intercourse is an action inscribed by the Creator with a two-fold meaning:

Unitive Dimension

Love-making is an expression of intimacy, a "two in one flesh" encounter, demanding a deep level of commitment and love for its natural fulfillment. It strengthens the bond of love between husband and wife.

Procreative Dimension

Marriage and sexual union are also given for the procreation of children. "Be fertile and multiply" (Gen 1:28). Union of egg and sperm produces new human life. Marital commitment and fidelity provide the only stable environment for sexual expression.
Sex is not a shameful thing, it is a divine gift, ordained to life, to love and to fruitfulness. (Familiaris Consortio, 1981). "Sex is, indeed, good, beautiful, and holy, but only at the service of and for the sake of eternal values." (Joseph de Torre, 1988)
What are Dehumanizing Acts against Sexuality?

Sexual activity outside of marriage is dehumanizing. Marriage is the only relationship in which sexual activity can achieve the full God-given meaning — a faithful, exclusive and total gift of oneself to another, open to love and the creation of new life.

1

Masturbation

An intrinsically and seriously disordered act because sexual activity is meant to be shared with one's spouse in the committed relationship of marriage. It frustrates the essential purpose of the sexual act.

2

Homosexual Acts

The purpose of the sexual faculty is the expression of married love and the generation of new life in the context of the family. To use the sexual faculty in a way in which the generation of new life is impossible is to misuse it and is intrinsically wrong.

3

Cohabitation / Fornication

Practice of an unmarried man and woman living together. "One does not try out a permanent commitment." There is no statistical evidence that testing produces any guarantees.

4

Prostitution

Sexual intercourse done merely for pleasure by people with no commitment. Outright disrespect for the person — the gift of sexuality is commodified and the person is used as a means or object of pleasure.

5

Contraception, Sterilization, Abortion

Acts that sever the unitive and procreative dimensions of the marital act; also includes some reproductive technologies including cloning and embryonic stem cell research.

What is Sexual Maturity & Sexual Health?

Sexual Maturity

The capacity to form a stable relationship with the opposite sex which is emotionally and physically satisfying.

Sexual Health

The integration of the somatic, emotional, intellectual and social aspects of sexual well-being, in ways that are positively enriching and that enhance personality, communication and love.
Key Definition — Chastity (CCC 2337–2338)
"Chastity" is the virtue of sexual self-control and is an aspect of the cardinal virtue of temperance. As a religious virtue, chastity motivates and enables us to use the gift of our sexuality in complete accordance with God's plan. Chastity makes possible the successful integration of sexuality within the person and thus the inner unity of the person in his bodily and spiritual being. (CCC 2337)
"The chaste person maintains the integrity of the powers of life and love placed in him. This integrity ensures the unity of the person; it is opposed to any behavior that would impair it." (CCC 2338)

"The future of humanity passes by way of the family."

— Pope John Paul II

What are the Contents of the Biblical Foundation of Marriage?
I. God Made Marriage II. God Made Marriage to Last for Life III. God Made Marriage to Be a Covenant
I. God Made Marriage
  • The institution of marriage began in the Garden of Eden.
  • Adam and Eve were created in a state of marriage.
  • God is the author of marriage — it was not invented by man.
  • "It is not good that the man should be alone." (Genesis 2:18)
  • The Bible opens with a marriage — between a man and a woman (Genesis), and closes with a wedding — between a Groom and Bride (Revelation).
  • God designed marriage as the foundational element for all human interaction and for society — including churches, schools, and governments.

What to Do in Marriage (Genesis 2:21–24)

  • Receive — Your mate as a gift from God (Gen 2:21–23)
  • Leave — Your parents physically, emotionally, and financially (Gen 2:24)
  • Cleave — To your mate / stick to (Gen 2:24)
  • One-flesh — The husband and wife become one / spiritual (Gen 2:24)

God's Purpose in Giving Marriage to All Mankind

  • To compensate for the weakness a man or woman has in being alone
  • To establish a faithful, monogamous relation, essential for the successful survival of society
  • To create the one-flesh relationship

Purposes of Marriage in the Old Testament

  • Mirror — God's image (Gen 1:26)
  • Mutually complete each other — Fill each other's gaps (Gen 2:18)
  • Multiply a godly legacy (Gen 1:28)
II. God Made Marriage to Last for Life

God made the marital union of one man and one woman last for their whole life. God's plan is for one man to love one woman. The only exception is through the death of one of the partners.

"What God has joined together, let no one separate."

— Mark 10:9 / Matthew 19:5
Jesus explains that divorce was granted by Moses as a concession because of the hardness of the hearts of men. God's opinion and teaching have not changed concerning divorce. Marriage was corrupted by sin (Genesis 3:16–19), but God's grace is available to whoever seeks it to stay married.

God's grace will do for us what we cannot do for ourselves. "If you will marry by the will of God, then you can remain married by the grace of God."

III. God Made Marriage to Be a Biblical Covenant

Covenant — a personal, solemn promise of faithful love that involves mutual commitments and creates a sacred relationship. The words are commitment and not convenience.

CovenantContract
Based on trust and unlimited responsibility between two partiesBased on distrust and limited liability
Cannot be broken if new circumstances occurCan be voided by mutual consent
Speaks more about love and commitment; lifelong, sacrifice, loveMore about giving and taking
Biblical model — unique to Christian MarriageLegal/secular model

Four Primary Biblical Roles of the Husband (Ephesians 5:23–27)

  • Lead (Eph 5:23)
  • Love (Eph 5:25)
  • Serve (Eph 5:26)
  • Provide for — Financially, emotionally, physically, and spiritually (Eph 5:26–27)

Four Primary Biblical Roles of the Wife (Ephesians 5:21–33)

  • Help (Eph 5:24)
  • Respect (Eph 5:33)
  • Love (Eph 5:33)
  • Submit — To your own husband (Eph 5:22)
Specific command for both husband and wife (Eph 5:21): "Submitting to one another out of reverence for Christ." This marriage covenant is not a give-and-take relationship but a total willingness to give and continue to give to each other, just like God keeps giving to us.

Theological Foundation of Marriage
Covenant Love · Sacrament · The Goods of Marriage
▼
What is the Theological Definition of Marriage?

"The matrimonial covenant, by which a man and a woman establish between themselves a partnership of the whole of life, which of its own very nature is ordered to the good of the spouses and the procreation and education of children, has been raised by Christ the Lord to the dignity of a sacrament between the baptized."

— Catechism of the Catholic Church 1601

The sacrament of marriage or matrimony is a covenant between a baptized man and woman — together they form an intimate communion of life and love.

A sacrament is a visible sign instituted by Christ to give grace. Christian marriage and the Christian family are deeply rooted in the Trinity.
Why is Marriage important from the theological perspective?
1

It is not a human institution but a divine one

God himself designed it.

2

God elevated it into a sacrament in Christ

A sacrament is a sign, instituted by Christ, to show God's love to the world and bring grace, joy and comfort to us.

3

At the heart of every marriage is love

To will the good of your spouse and family.

Who is the "minister" in the Sacrament of Marriage?
In this Sacrament, it is the couple who are the ministers of the Sacrament, and the priest or deacon who presides is the Church's witness to the Sacrament.
Marriage is not only a RIGHT but a mutual GIFT of two persons. It is a covenant which establishes between husband and wife a "partnership of the whole life" in which they "mutually hand over and accept each other." (Canon Law 1055)
What are the Purposes of Matrimony?

Married love has both unitive and procreative purposes of marriage.

"By its very nature the institution of marriage and married love is ordered to the procreation and education of the offspring and it is in them that it finds its crowning glory."

— CCC 1652 / GS 48

FREE

Jesus modeled through His passion and death what free love really means.

TOTAL

As Jesus gave us all that He had, He shows us what total love really looks like.

FAITHFUL

Jesus was faithful to His Father's will and faithful to each one of us.

FRUITFUL

There was never a more fruitful life and death than that of Jesus Christ. His death brings new life to humanity.
What are the Goods (Bona) of Marriage? — St. Augustine

The expression "goods" (bona) of marriage originated with St. Augustine:

proles Offspring
fides Chaste Fidelity
sacramentum Unbreakable Bond
A

Offspring (proles)

The fruitfulness of the conjugal union fulfills man's and woman's longing for self-perpetuation and the perpetuation, in offspring, of conjugal love.

B

Chaste Fidelity (fides)

Refers to the exclusive fidelity of the marital relationship of one man with one woman. Expresses the commitment to a faithful and exclusive love in marriage.

C

Unbreakable Bond (sacramentum)

This unity demands permanence (indissolubility) for the entire life of the married couple. The union is so profound and total that only death can dissolve it.

These elements — procreation, fidelity and sacrament — form the basis of the Church's understanding of marriage even today.

What is Marriage according to Canon Law? (Canon 1055)

"Marriage is a covenant, by which a man and a woman establish between themselves a partnership of their whole life, and which of its own very nature is ordered to the well-being of the spouses and to the procreation and upbringing of children. It has, between the baptized, been raised by Christ the Lord to the dignity of a sacrament."

— Canon 1055, Code of Canon Law
How does Canon Law compare to Civil Law on Marriage?
Civil Law (Law of the Land)Canon Law (Laws of the Church — "rule")
Marriage is a legal agreement (contract)Marriage is a sacrament — a bonding of two people
Marriage License issued by City HallCivil and Church license issued by priest
Judge or Justice of the Peace performs ceremonyPriest performs ceremony; Church wedding with blessing before family and friends
Service: exchange of vows; Legal agreementThe Mass: Eucharistic Celebration and exchange of vows

DIVORCE — legal separation; division of propertyANNULMENT — not divorce; necessary to marry in the Church again
What are Impediments to Matrimony?

Impediments to matrimony are certain restrictions imposed by the law of God and of the Church which render a marriage contract invalid or unlawful if such restrictions are violated when entering a marriage.

Diriment (Annulling/Nullifying) ImpedimentsProhibitive (Hindering) Impediments
Render an attempted marriage altogether null and void, invalid.Render a marriage unlawful, illicit, but valid.
Dispensations are only rarely granted.The couple are married though unlawfully.
Without dispensation, there is NO marriage.The marriage cannot be dissolved.
When impediments exist, the Church either completely forbids the administration of the sacrament, or requires special assurances that the dangers may be reduced as far as possible.
What are the Diriment Impediments?
Physical Defect
1

Lack of Age

Boys under sixteen (16); Girls under fourteen (14).

2

Impotence

Antecedent (exists prior to marriage) and perpetual (cannot be corrected) impotence, whether absolute (with any person) or relative (with particular individuals), invalidates matrimony by its very nature. Sterility neither forbids nor invalidates marriage. The Church cannot dispense from impotence. (Canon 1084)

Moral Bond
3

Existing Marriage Bond

One who is already married cannot marry again while the other party is still living. This impediment is never given a dispensation. If someone believing his spouse dead contracts another marriage, he must immediately leave the second spouse if the first be discovered living.

4

Disparity of Worship

Marriage between a Catholic and an unbaptized person (e.g., Mohammedans, Jews, Buddhists, Shintoists, infidels). Without dispensation, renders marriage invalid, null, and not a marriage.

5

Sacred Orders

Persons in holy orders invalidly attempt matrimony (Canon 1086). The law of celibacy and the diriment impediment of sacred orders binds since deaconship. This is an impediment of ecclesiastical law.

6

Public Perpetual Vows of Chastity

Men who have received major orders, monks, and nuns who have taken a solemn vow of chastity cannot contract a valid marriage.

Crime
7

Coercion

Contracting parties must give consent freely. If undue stress is brought — through abduction, violence, fear of bodily injury, of being disinherited, etc. — the impediment is annulling and there is no marriage.

8

Crime

A person who, for the purpose of entering marriage with a certain person, has brought about the death of that person's spouse or one's own spouse, invalidly attempts such marriage. (Canon 1090)

Relationship
9

Consanguinity (Blood Relationship)

The bond that unites persons of the same blood. Direct line: marriage is invalid between all ancestors and descendants. Collateral line: marriage is invalid up to and including the 4th degree. Degrees: siblings = 2nd; uncle-nephew = 3rd; first cousins = 4th; second cousins = 6th (collateral).

10

Affinity

Relationship by marriage (in-laws). The survivor cannot marry the blood relations of his dead spouse. In the direct line (e.g., stepdaughter, mother-in-law) — no dispensation is granted. In the collateral line — extends to the 2nd degree, and may be dispensed. (Canon 1092)

11

Public Decency

Arises from an invalid marriage after common life has been established, or from notorious and public concubinage (cohabitation of persons not legally married).

12

Spiritual Affinity

Without dispensations, sponsors in Baptism cannot marry their godchildren. Example: a lay person cannot marry the person whom he administered lay baptism.

13

Legal Relationship

Those who by civil law are incapable of contracting marriage by reason of a legal relation arising from adoption are also incapable, by Church law, of a valid marriage.

How does an Annulment differ from a Divorce?
DivorceAnnulment
Legal termination of a marriage; ends or cancels a valid marriage; returns both parties to single statusTurns a marriage null and void by proving that a man and a woman were never united in marriage as husband and wife
One must be separated definitively from a spouse for at least one year before filing an annulment case. An annulment is filed ordinarily when a marriage has become hopelessly irremediable or gravely intolerable.
What are the Grounds for Annulment due to Defect/Lack of Consent?
A

Lack of Sufficient Use of Reason (Canon 1095, 1°)

Mental retardation, very low IQ — morons, insanity, and the like.

B

Lack of Due Discretion — LDD (Canon 1095, 2°)

Lack of either full will or full knowledge at the moment of consent ("I do").

Common Causes:

  • Immaturity — below 23 (female) or 25 (male) does not usually possess needed maturity. "Had I known better or been more mature/knowledgeable, I would not have consented."
  • Pressure to Marry — external or internal, physical or psychological, moral or spiritual, parental or social (e.g., premarital pregnancy, shotgun marriage, fear of embarrassment, unhappy home, rebounding). "Had I not been pressured/forced, I would not have consented."
  • Short Courtship — does not enable partners to know each other enough for a lifetime partnership. One year is not enough. "Had I been given enough time to know him/her better, I would not have consented."
  • Impeded Knowledge — hidden venereal disease, other physical or psycho-emotional defects, hiding one's real status or essential background. "Had I known that he/she was like that, I would never have consented."
C

Lack of Due Competence — LDC (Canon 1095, 3°) — Incapacity to Assume Essential Obligations

One may understand what marriage entails but not possess the needed ability to assume or fulfill its essential obligations, due to psychological defects.

Examples: manic-addict (sex, kleptomania), manic-depressive (bipolar), reactive depression, spouse batterer (physical/sexual/verbal/psycho-emotional), sexual neurosis, schizoid, schizophrenic, paranoid, histrionic, narcissistic, antisocial, borderline personality, alcoholism, substance-drug dependence, homosexuality, affective immaturity, hypersexuality (satyriasis/nymphomania), chronic/congenital liars, and the like.
Two Common Errors:
1. A "capable" partner marries the "psychologically incapable" with hope of changing them. No way! No one can change anyone.
2. A "capable" spouse marries the "psychologically incapable" out of pity. No way! Pity is pity; true consent to marriage as lifetime partnership is another.

What is Canon Law?

"Canon" comes from the Greek kanon — meaning straight rod, measuring stick, or rule.

Canon Law is the oldest and continuously functioning legal system in the Western world. It is the internal legal system of the Catholic Church.

The supreme law of the Church is the salvation of souls — salus animarum. The purpose of Canon Law is to create order in the Church so that she organizes and governs herself. Ubi societas ibi lex — "Where there is a society, there is law."
Of all the seven sacraments, Matrimony has been the most widely challenged in the history of the Catholic Church. Over the centuries it has been the single principal cause of whole nations breaking with the Catholic Church — the Orthodox Churches in the 13th century, and Protestantism in the 16th century.
What is the Canon Law Definition of Marriage? (Canon 1055-1)

"The matrimonial covenant, by which a man and a woman establishes between themselves the partnership of the whole of life and which is ordered by its nature to the good of the spouses and the procreation and education of offspring, has been raised by Christ the Lord to the dignity of a sacrament between the baptized."

— Canon 1055-1

1. Covenant

An agreement between God and his people involving promises and commitment — not a mere contract. Lifelong commitment, sacrifice, love.

2. Partnership of Whole Life

No individualistic attitude — you share all joys and sorrows, in sickness and in health, in good times and in bad, until death. Consortio.

3. Good of the Spouses

Well-being of husband and wife: mutual welfare, mutual support, mutual help, mutual assistance — material, intellectual, spiritual, and emotional well-being.

4. Procreation & Education of Offspring

No one should exclude this right of children from marital life. "It is in them that it finds its crowning glory." (CCC 1652)
What makes a Valid Marriage?

A valid marriage requires one man and one woman, at least one of whom is a baptized Latin Catholic, who:

Are properly disposed Have fulfilled required preparation Are free from impediments Have the required intentions

Required Intentions

  • Permanence
  • Exclusivity / Fidelity
  • Good of the spouses
  • Procreation
  • Sacramentality (among the baptized)

Parties need to understand and intend these things, or at least not exclude them from their consent.

What is the Required Preparation for Marriage?
1

Pre-Marital Inventory

Do NOT set a wedding date until sure of the parties' freedom to marry.

2

Formation Process

Pre-Cana, Engagement Encounter, and similar programs.

3

Pastoral Meetings

Beginning 6 months prior to the wedding.

Required Documents

  • Baptismal certificate for Catholics (within 6 months, with notations)
  • Proof of formation process
  • Marriage license
The pastor of the Catholic party/parties is responsible for preparation. The pastor of the parish where marriage will be celebrated is responsible for ensuring required preparation is complete before allowing marriage in his parish. The marriage file and record is kept at the parish where marriage takes place.
Who performs the marriage, and how is it celebrated?
Parties marry each other — they are the ministers of marriage. An authorized priest or deacon assists by asking for and receiving consent/vows of both parties before two witnesses.

Where & When?

  • Marriages celebrated in the parish church of one of the Catholic parties.
  • Pastor can grant permission for marriage in a secondary church or another parish.
  • Local Ordinary can grant permission for marriage outside a Catholic church (e.g., Protestant church in a mixed marriage).
  • Marriages can be celebrated any day but Sunday, Good Friday, and Holy Saturday. Bishop can grant permission for a Sunday wedding.
